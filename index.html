Ways to play

Fresh off the pack

Pack Wars

  • works best with two players
  • one booster per player
  • chaotic

Players open one booster each (from the same set) and without looking at the cards remove chuff. Then shuffle in three of each basic land types and play with that deck.

No mulligans are allowed. The idea is that players don’t know what’s in their decks.

Winchester Draft

  • works best with two players; up to four
  • three boosters per player
  • deck building
  • common pool of cards (disadventage for beginners)

Open three boosters for each player and without looking at the cards remove chuff. Shuffle all of the cards together and put them in the middle of the table. Take four cards and put them face up creating four piles (for now one card per pile). Players take turns picking one pile to keep. After each player chooses, add one card to each of the piles. Continue until all cards are picked.

Players will have to balance picking up piles with more cards vs piles with less (but better) cards.

Then players spend some time building decks (40+ cards) out of what they got. They can add any basic lands they need.

Read more about Winchester Draft

Sealed

  • can be made into a small tournament
  • six booster per player
  • deck building

Each player opens six boosters from the same set and spend some time aside creating decks (40+ cards) out of the cards they got. They can add any basic lands they need.

Read more about how we do it

Draft

  • traditionally eight players, could be made with less
  • three booster per player
  • deck building
  • common pool of cards (disadventage for beginners)

Players sit in a circle. Every player opens a pack, removes chuff and chooses one card to keep, then passes the pack to the right. After they run out of cards, they open next booster, but this time pass in the other direction. Repeat until all cards are drafted.

Then players spend some time building decks (40+ cards) out of what they got. They can add any basic lands they need.

Bring your own deck

Jumpstart

  • works best with two players
  • casual
  • beginner friendly
  • fast
  • 40 cards

Product from Wizards in form of 20–card packs that include lands. You can grab two of them, combine and have a working simple deck.

When you have sealed packs, it’s fun to mix them without looking and play. After that you can pick and choose or grab them at random.

Tip: Avoid combining two half–decks with the same colour to make separating them after the game easier.

There are different kinds of Jumpstarts.

Jumpstart originally came out in 2020 (called just “Jumpstart”). Since then Wizards released Jumpstart products for most of their sets, but they are worse than the original product. “Jumpstart 2023” is another Jumpstart product that’s not attached to any set and is also good.

Regular play

  • works best with two players
  • traditional
  • 60 cards

The OG way to play. Decks have 60 cards, with up to 4 copies of each card (except for basic lands).

There are multiple formats (Standard, Modern, Legacy, Vintage, …) that differ in which cards are allowed to use. For casual games you don’t have to worry about that, as long as you are sure that decks are on a similar power level.

Commander

  • works best for four players
  • 100 cards
  • long games

Format designed for longer games with more players, where politiking plays a bigger role. Decks have 100 cards, with no duplicates allowed (except for basic lands). One of the cards is the “commander” that lives outside of the deck (“command zone”) and can be summoned at any time. Usually remaining 99 cards synergise in some way with your commander. When commander leaves battlefield you can put it back in “command zone” and summon it again, but it costs two mana of any colour more each time. Players start with 40 life. When any commander deals total of 21 damage to a player (“commander damage”), that player loses.

In most cases it’se self–balancing. Even if decks are of different power levels, players naturaly attack the strongest player first.

Probably the most popular format nowadays.

Accessories

Things you might want in addition to cards.

Sleeves

  • Cheapest option are Ultra Pro Soft Penny Sleeves. They wear off relatively quickly and stick to each other when shuffling.
  • My favourite ones are KMC Hyper Mat (transparent). You can also get them in different colours.
  • Dragon Shield Matte are also good and popular.

Inner sleeves

For even better protection, you can double–sleeve your cards preventing dirt from coming in from the top of the sleeve. KMC Perfect Sized are my favourite ones.

Deck boxes

  • I like Ultimate Guard Boulder for being very compact, sturdy and water tight.
  • Many people seem to like Ultra Pro Tower.
  • Ultra Pro Solid Black Deckbox is a classic, cheap option.

Storage

You could go fancy, but cheapest option is a simple cardboard storage box for 550 cards. Many other sizes are available.

Other

There are many other things like playmats, dice, life counters and more. Spend as little or as much as you want. 🙂

Terms

booster

A pack of random cards. There are few different types of boosters, but the ones that you should be focusing on are called “Draft Booster” or “Play Booster”. Other types of boosters are designed more for collecting than playing.

chuff

Any cards in a booster pack that don’t go into your deck: tokens, ads, art cards, rules, covers etc.

precon

Preconstructed decks sold by Wizards of the Coast.

mulligan

When you draw your starting hand and don’t like what you see, you can mulligan — i.e. shuffle your hand back into the deck, draw new seven cards, then choose one card and put it on the bottom of your library. You can repeat it multiple times, but each time you do, you need to put one additional card on the bottom of the library.

In some situations you can mulligan for free — i.e. without having to bottom a card:

  • first mulligan, when playing multi–player game (e.g. commander),
  • if both players are mulliganing and they agree to,
  • as a house rule, when you draw all lands or no lands,
  • as a house rule, if you draw just one land or just one non–land.

There’s a nice video about mulligans by Tolarian Community College.
